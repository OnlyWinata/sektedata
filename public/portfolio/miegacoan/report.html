Mie Gacoan operates 557 outlets across 34 provinces — each with its own lease agreement, building permit, halal certificate, and potential legal disputes. This dashboard answers the 3 strategic questions that the Head of Legal needs answered every morning: which branches are at risk, where are the risks concentrated, and what needs action today.

Q1 — HEAD OF LEGAL

Which branches have critical compliance issues?

The map below shows dispute risk by province. Red provinces (Jakarta, Jawa Barat, Jawa Timur, Banten) have the highest concentration of legal disputes — these 4 provinces alone account for 60% of all high-risk cases despite having 55% of outlets.

Tingkat_Sengketa Low High (Butuh Eskalasi) Medium
Java island carries 80% of high-dispute cases. Dedicated legal counsel for Jabodetabek alone would reduce dispute resolution time by est. 40% and save Rp 200-300M in annual legal fees.
Q2 — CFO · LEGAL TEAM

Where are the lease expiration risks concentrated?

Lease renewals involve Rp 150-800M per outlet per year. The chart below shows how many outlets fall into each urgency bucket. The red bars represent branches that need action within 90 days — or face closure.

Q3 — VP OPERATIONS · LEGAL

What's the geographic distribution of disputes?

Understanding where disputes cluster helps identify systemic issues — is it a region, a property type, or a landlord pattern?

Dispute Level Distribution
Outlets by Province (Top 10)
70% of outlets have low dispute levels — the network is generally healthy. But the 10% with high disputes are overwhelmingly in urban Java. Jawa Timur has the most outlets (120) but Jakarta has the highest dispute rate per outlet. This suggests Jakarta's property market dynamics (not outlet count) drive legal risk.
