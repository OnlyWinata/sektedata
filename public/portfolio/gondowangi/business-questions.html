Strategic Questions & Analysis Framework

Before diving into data, define what questions matter most — and for whom.

As a Sales Data Analyst at Gondowangi, the job isn't just making charts — it's answering the questions that drive business decisions. Here are the 3 strategic questions this analysis addresses.

Strategic Questions

Q1: How are Gondowangi brands performing vs competitors in each category?

👤 Stakeholder: VP of Sales · Head of Marketing

With 4 brands across 8 categories, management needs to know where Gondowangi is winning and where it's losing. Market share by category tells us which brands need more investment and which categories to deprioritize.

Q2: Which regions are underperforming relative to their potential?

👤 Stakeholder: Regional Sales Managers · VP of Distribution

Gondowangi distributes across 10 regions in Indonesia. Some regions may have high population but low sales penetration — these are growth opportunities.

Q3: What's the optimal channel mix for maximizing revenue and margin?

👤 Stakeholder: CFO · Head of E-Commerce · Trade Marketing

With 6 sales channels, each has different margin profiles. E-commerce is growing fast but comes with platform fees. The question is: where should Gondowangi invest?

💡 Senior vs Junior Thinking

A junior analyst would jump straight to "let me make a bar chart of revenue by brand." A senior analyst asks: "Who needs this information, what decision will they make with it, and what's the business impact?" Every chart in this portfolio exists because a stakeholder needs it to make a specific decision.
